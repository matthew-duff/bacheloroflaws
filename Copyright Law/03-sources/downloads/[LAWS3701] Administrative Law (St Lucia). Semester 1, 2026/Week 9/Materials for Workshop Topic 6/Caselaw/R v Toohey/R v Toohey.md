The distinction has frequently been drawn in this area of
discourse between powers which have their origin in the prerogatives
of the Crown, and those which are conferred by statute, the latter
being said to be more susceptible of judicial review that the
former.
It may well be true that the limits of a statutory power can
be more readily discerned from the Act conferring the power.
The
distinction is well illustrated in the historical survey which Mason J.
has undertaken in his reasons for judgment in this case, and to which

"For the prerogative consisting ... in the discretionary power

if that discretionary power be abused to the public detriment,
