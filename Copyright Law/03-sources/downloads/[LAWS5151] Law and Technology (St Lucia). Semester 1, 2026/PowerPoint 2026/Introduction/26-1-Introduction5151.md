# Slide 24

Do lawyers have a duty of technological competence? Worsoff v MTCC 1168 (2021) ONSC 6493

32. As to the balance of convenience and any other relevant matters, Mr. Marcovitch submitted that just because virtual procedures are “easier and more convenient” does not overcome the presumption that examination in person is the best way to examine a witness. Au contraire I say. Efficiency, affordability, and enhanced access to justice trump counsels’ comfort and presumptions every time. With the current pace of change, everyone has to keep learning technology. Counsel and the court alike have a duty of technological competency in my respectful view. Older judges and counsel may be behind younger counsel and the rest of society who use computers with greater regularity and sophistication than we do. But everyone in the civil litigation system in Ontario has had to learn to use the Civil Submissions Online portal and Caselines for example. Technological change affects everyone. Once upon a time, I had to learn how to use a Gestetner (Google it) and then a fax machine. I do not accept that in person is just “better”. It can be in some cases. But if counsel just prefers it because he or she is more comfortable with it, ought we to reject the printer because I liked my Gestetner (and Word Perfect for that matter)? The balance of convenience favours easier and more convenient processes with accompanying cost savings. [Emphasis added.]

Misconceptions

THE DEEP AND DARK WEB ARE THE SAME THING
Deep web - sites not indexed by search engines (Blackboard). Clubs, Business, Government, Medical sites, login portals, journals, legal and financial records, Paid sites, education, etc.  IP issues, Confidentiality, privacy issues, commercial value. etc
Dark web - purposely hidden - usually to avoid scrutiny by law enforcement, and other entities. Requires specialised software such as Tor. Illegal purposes, but may be legitimate; such as free speech, criticise government or corporations, whistleblowing etc.
Many hidden services host harmful content - hacking forums for exploits and malware, selling drugs, stolen personal information, and counterfeit goods, child sexual exploitation and abuse material, 
CYBERCRIME ONLY HAPPENS ON THE DARK WEB
YOU ARE ANONYMOUS ON THE DARK WEB
The dark web is considerably more anonymous than browsing the clear web, however many vulnerabilities exist.

# Slide 56

Dark Web?

Dark Web - cluster of nodes and networks called “darknets.” Peer-to-peer networks small and big, including Tor and Freenet
Need specialised software – eg Tor browser:
Developed 1990s for US intelligence communications - is the preferred method to access Dark Web
The Onion Router (TOR) 3 layers of encryption and specific internet traffic routing mechanism to ensure robust anonymity. Combines strong layers of encryption with ability to randomly bounce internet traffic through the Tor relays.
This protect users’ identities, online activities and location - most are logged into a Virtual Private Network (VPN)
Not necessarily illegal
