LAWS5206: Copyright Law

Downloading copyright material without the consent of the copyright owner is a breach of the owner’s copyright
BUT such downloading for personal use is (generally) not a crime and is not properly regarded (in a legal sense) as “stealing”:
No reference to copyright in the Criminal Code
Offences in the Copyright Act are (largely) limited to commercial scale operations and public performances – see Part V, Division 5 (ss 132AA to 133B)
Note however that it is an indictable offence to gain unauthorised access to a subscription service: s 135 ASI 
The copyright owner will have a civil claim against any such unauthorised downloader

Copyright only protects the types of subject matter identified in the Copyright Act.  Nothing else!

# Slide 50

1. Originality (Part III); Not copied (Part IV)
The relevant subject matter must originate from the author or creator
2. Expression v facts / ideas
Copyright does NOT protect pure information, facts or ideas.  
It only protects the particular expression of that information or those ideas.
3. Connection to Australia – by direct connection or via a treaty
4. Material form
Copyright (generally) does NOT protect subject matter unless it has been reduced to a material form
5. Substantiality
Copyright does NOT protect subject matter that is too insubstantial – e.g. single words, simple phrases

Infringement requires:
the taking of a substantial part of the relevant work or other subject matter
that the part taken is objectively similar to the copyright work
that there was actual copying (direct or indirect, conscious or unconscious)

Each of these must be carefully considered when deciding whether infringement has occurred

LAWS5206: Copyright Law

# Slide 59

IF you can successfully show that:
Copyright subsists in the relevant work or subject matter and
You are the owner of that copyright and
One or more of your exclusive rights has been exercised by someone else without your permission and
None of the defences or exceptions apply
THEN you have a good claim for copyright infringement.
