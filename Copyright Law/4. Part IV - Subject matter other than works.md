Unlike Part III "works," Part IV subject matter (often called "neighbouring rights" or "related rights") does not require **originality** in the authorial sense. These are typically industrial or non-authorial productions.

> [!INFO] Key Distinction
> Part IV protection is generally **narrower** than Part III. It is often restricted to protection against literal and technical copying of the specific embodiment (the recording, the film, or the broadcast).

---

## 🎬 1. Cinematograph Films
Defined in **s 10(1)** as the aggregate of visual images embodied in an article or thing.

### 🔍 Key Principles
- **Authorship vs. Ownership:** Films involve many contributors. Under Part IV, the "maker" (usually the producer) is the initial owner.
- **Overlap with Part III:** A film often embodies multiple Part III works:
    - 📄 **Literary:** The screenplay or script.
    - 🎭 **Dramatic:** The choreographed scenes.
    - 🎨 **Artistic:** Depictions of buildings, paintings, or sculptures within the film.
- **Scope of Protection:**
    - 🎮 **Computer Games:** May be protected as "films" where they involve a sequence of images (*[[Sega & Avel v Galaxy Electronics*).
    - 🎰 **Poker Machines:** Generally held *not* to be films.
    - 📡 **Live Events:** Transmissions of live events (e.g., a live sports broadcast) are not "films" because they are not yet "embodied" in a material form.

---

## 🔊 2. Sound Recordings
The aggregate of sounds embodied in a record (**s 10(1)**).

- **Nature of Right:** Protects the specific recording (the "master"), not the underlying musical work or lyrics (which are Part III works).
- **Performers' Rights:** Since the US-Australia FTA (2004), sound performers have gained economic and moral rights in the recordings of their performances.

---

## 📡 3. Broadcasts
Copyright subsists in television and sound broadcasts made from a place in Australia by a licensee under the *Broadcasting Services Act*.

> [!NOTE] The Panel Litigation (*Network Ten v LLP*)
> This case clarified the "substantial part" of a broadcast:
> - ❌ Copyright does **not** subsist in each and every individual moment or frame.
> - ✅ Copyright subsists in each **program** and **advertisement**.
> - ❌ Individual program segments (brief clips) may not constitute a "substantial part" of the broadcast.

---

## 📖 4. Published Editions of Works
This category is unique as it protects the **typographical arrangement** of a published edition.

- **Purpose:** To prevent competitors from simply photocopying or "facsimile" copying a publisher's layout.
- **Scope:** Protection is limited to the layout, not the content of the works within it.
- **Duration:** Typically a shorter term (25 years from the end of the year of first publication).
