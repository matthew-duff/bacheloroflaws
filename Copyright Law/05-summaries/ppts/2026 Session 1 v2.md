**Citation**: LAWS5206: Copyright Law, Session One (28 February 2026), University of Queensland.

**Facts**: 
These are the introductory lecture slides for the LAWS5206 Copyright Law course in 2026. They provide an overview of the course structure, assessment, and a "crash course" in Australian copyright law. Key topics include the definition of intellectual property, the statutory basis of copyright in Australia (*Copyright Act 1968* (Cth)), and the distinction between Part III "Works" and Part IV "Subject Matter Other Than Works".

**Issue**: 
What are the fundamental principles and statutory frameworks governing copyright law in Australia?

**Held**: 
Copyright is a bundle of exclusive economic and moral rights conferred by statute (*Copyright Act 1968* (Cth)). It is territorial, finite in duration, and requires no registration. Protection is limited to specific categories of subject matter that meet prerequisites such as originality (for works), material form, and connection to Australia.

**Reasoning**: 
*   **Statutory Basis**: Copyright exists only by virtue of the *Copyright Act 1968* (Cth); there is no common law copyright.
*   **Categorisation**: Subject matter is strictly divided into Part III (Literary, Dramatic, Musical, and Artistic works) and Part IV (Sound recordings, Films, Broadcasts, and Published editions).
*   **Infringement**: Occurs when an exclusive right is exercised without permission in relation to a "substantial part" of the protected material, involving objective similarity and actual copying.
*   **Remedies**: Available under s 115, including injunctions, damages, and accounts of profits.

**Significance**: 
The slides establish the "6 Easy Steps" framework for copyright analysis: 
1. Identify the material.
2. Identify owners/licensees.
3. Identify exclusive rights.
4. Identify infringement.
5. Identify defences/exceptions.
6. Consider remedies.
This provides the foundational methodology used throughout the course.
