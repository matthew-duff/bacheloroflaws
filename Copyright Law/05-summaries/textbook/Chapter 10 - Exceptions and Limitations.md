# Chapter 10 - Exceptions and Limitations

## Overview
Chapter 10 examines the various free exceptions to copyright infringement under the *Copyright Act 1968* (Cth). These exceptions are essential for balancing the exclusive rights of copyright owners with broader social, economic, and policy imperatives. Unlike statutory licences (Chapter 9), these exceptions do not require remuneration and typically operate as defences to infringement.

### Key Exceptions
1. **Implied Licences (10.2)**
   - Not a true licence but an estoppel-like mechanism to prevent detriment to third parties who have a legitimate expectation to use copyright material (e.g., architectural plans purchased with land).
   - **Criterion**: Must be a "necessity" to protect accrued rights (*Copyright Agency v NSW*; *Acohs v Ucorp*).
   - Onus of proof rests on the defendant.

2. **Fair Dealing (10.3)**
   - The most significant category of exceptions, requiring the use to be for a specific purpose and to be "fair".
   - **Purposes**: 
     - Research or study (with "deemed fairness" quantities for works).
     - Criticism or review (interpreted liberally to include contextual critique: *Pro Sieben*).
     - Parody or satire (added in 2006; targets the source or uses it as a weapon: *AGL v Greenpeace*).
     - Reporting of news.
     - Professional legal advice.
     - Access for persons with a disability (Part IVA).
   - **Fairness Factors**: Purpose/character, nature of the material, commercial availability, effect on the market, and substantiality of the part taken.

3. **Artistic Works Exceptions (10.4 - 10.5)**
   - **Miscellaneous (Div 7)**: Includes "incidental" inclusion in films/broadcasts, and photography of sculptures/buildings in public places.
   - **Copyright/Design Overlap (Div 8)**: Prevents dual protection for mass-produced 3D products. If a "corresponding design" (shape/configuration) is industrially applied (usually >50 articles) or registered, copyright in the underlying 2D drawing cannot be used to stop others from making the 3D product.
   - **Exception**: Does not apply to works of artistic craftsmanship or "flat" designs (pattern/ornamentation).

4. **Computer Programs (10.6)**
   - Specific exceptions in Division 4A for:
     - Incidental/automatic reproduction during normal use.
     - Back-up copies and data security.
     - Interoperability (permitting decompilation to achieve connection with other software: *Google v Oracle* (US context)).
     - Error correction and security testing.
   - **Contractual Override**: Section 47H nullifies contracts that attempt to exclude these software exceptions.

5. **Private Use (10.7)**
   - **Time-shifting**: Recording broadcasts for later viewing (*Optus TV Now* case limited this to individuals, not commercial services).
   - **Format-shifting**: Permitted for books, newspapers, photographs, and sound recordings (e.g., ripping a CD to an MP3 player), but highly restricted for films (only analogue to digital).

6. **Other Targeted Exceptions (10.8)**
   - Includes specific provisions for libraries, archives, education (s 200AB "three-step test" safety net), and government use.

## Significance
This chapter is critical for understanding the "user rights" side of copyright law. It defines the boundaries of infringement and ensures that copyright does not stifle innovation (interoperability), free speech (criticism/parody), or everyday activities (private use). The complexity of the **Copyright/Design Overlap** and the evolving nature of **Fair Dealing** (especially the shift toward more flexible interpretations) are central themes in modern Australian copyright litigation.
