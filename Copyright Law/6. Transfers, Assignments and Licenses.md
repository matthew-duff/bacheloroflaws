## Orphan works
"You can't take it with you" - play by moss hart and George Kaufman

- Orphan works are works where the creator is not identifiable, making obtaining permission very difficult.

> We seem to have general agreement that in the case of a true orphan work, where there is no copyright owner and therefore no beneficiary of the copyright term, it does not further the objectives of the copyright system to deny use of the work, sometimes for decades. In other words, it is not good policy to protect a copyright when there is no evidence of a copyright owner
