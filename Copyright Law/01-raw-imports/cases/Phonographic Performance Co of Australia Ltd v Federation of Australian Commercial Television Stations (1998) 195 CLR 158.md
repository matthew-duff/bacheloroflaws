Section 85(1 )(c) of the Copyright Act 1968 (Cth) provided that
copyright in relation to a sound recording included the exclusive right to
broadcast the recording. Section 10 defined sound recording to mean
"the aggregate of the sounds embodied in a record". and record to mean
"a disc. tape, paper or other device in which sounds are embodied".
Section 86(c) provided that copyright in relation to a cinematograph film
included the exclusive right to broadcast the film. Section 10 defined
cinematograph film to mean the aggregate of visual images embodied in
an article or thing capable by use of that article or thing of being shown
as a moving picture or of being embodied in another article or thing by
the use of which it can be shown; and to include the aggregate of the
sounds embodied in a sound-track associated with such visual images.
Section 23(1) provided that. for the purposes of the Act. "sounds
embodied in a sound-track associated with visual images forming part of
cinematograph film shall be deemed not to be a sound recording".
Sound-track was defined by s 10. in relation to the visual images forming
part of a cinematograph film, to mean the part of an article or thing in
which visual images were embodied in which sounds were embodied. or
a disc. tape or other device in which sounds were embodied and which
was made available by the maker of the film for use in conjunction with
the article or thing in which the visual images were embodied. Section 24
provided that "[flor the purposes of this Act. sounds or visual images
shall be taken to have been embodied in an article or thing if the article
or thing has been so treated in relation to those sounds or visual images
that [they] are capable. with or without the aid of some other device. of
being reproduced from the article or thing."

the identification, for the purposes of s 85, of the medium by which
the aggregate of sounds in the sound recording which it is said has
been broadcast was recorded.
25

is a construction that is not warranted by the words of the Act and is a
construction that would lead to obviously absurd results for it would
mean that upon the use of a sound recording in the sound-track of a
cinematograph film, copyright in a sound recording would cease. That
construction may therefore be put aside.

sounds embodied in a sound-track. To say that sounds embodied in
that particular form of record are not (or "shall be deemed not to be")
a sound recording, addresses a question different from that posed by
s 85.
