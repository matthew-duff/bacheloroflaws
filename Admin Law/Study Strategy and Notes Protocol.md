# Admin Law: Study Strategy and Notes Protocol

## 🎯 Objectives
This document outlines the strategy for processing materials, taking effective notes, and preparing for the two main assessments in Admin Law:
1. **Essay / Critique (40%)**
2. **End-of-semester Examination (60%)**

---

## 📂 Directory Structure & Material Utilization
To keep everything organized, we should establish a clear directory structure:

*   `/sources/` - Store all raw PDFs, cases, and reading materials here.
*   `/summaries/` - Contains your parsed, finalized notes for each topic/week.
*   `/scaffolds/` - Contains step-by-step problem-solving guides (IRAC/MIRAC) for the final exam.
*   `/essay_prep/` - Contains materials, arguments, and drafts specifically for the essay/critique.

### How to Utilize Materials
1.  **Case Law**: Admin law is heavily case-based (e.g., *Kioa v West*, *Minister for Immigration vs Li*). Focus on changes in judicial reasoning, grounds of review, and jurisdictional error.
2.  **Statute Frameworks**: Note down the *Administrative Decisions (Judicial Review) Act 1977* (ADJR Act) vs common law review. Know the differences in grounds and remedies.
3.  **Academic Articles**: Use these extensively for your Essay/Critique. They provide the critical lens necessary for high marks.

---

## 📝 Note-Taking Strategy

### The "Three-Pass" Note System

1.  **Pass 1: Weekly Summaries**
    *   Create a markdown file for each topic/week (e.g., `Week 3 - Procedural Fairness.md`).
    *   Synthesize lecture notes, case summaries, and statutory frameworks.
    *   *Format:* Use headings and highlight the test established in each key case.

2.  **Pass 2: Critical/Policy Notes (Essay Prep)**
    *   As you read, keep a separate document or tag within summaries for recurring critiques of current admin law.
    *   *Format:* Note down debates (e.g., merits vs judicial review, expanding grounds of unreasonableness).

3.  **Pass 3: Exam Scaffolds**
    *   Synthesize the Weekly Summaries into highly structured "scaffolds" for problem questions.
    *   *Format:* Step-by-step jurisdictional guides. E.g., "Step 1: Does the ADJR Act apply? -> Decision of an administrative character? -> Made under an enactment?"

---

## ✍️ Preparation for Essay / Critique (40%)
The essay tests your ability to critically analyze the law, its application, and its underlying policy.

*   **Focus Areas:** Theoretical debates, coherence of case law, and law reform.
*   **Preparation Method:**
    *   **Literature Mapping:** Map out the different academic viewpoints on your chosen topic.
    *   **Drafting Early:** Create an outline well before the April due date to ensure flow and logical consistency.

---

## ✍️ Preparation for End-of-semester Examination (60%)
The exam will likely be a complex problem question requiring you to advise a client on their prospects for judicial or merits review.

*   **Focus Areas:** Issue spotting, strict application of the ADJR Act or common law tests, remedies.
*   **Preparation Method:**
    *   **Navigation:** Build comprehensive flowcharts (e.g., ADJR vs Common Law vs State routes).
    *   **Practice Exams:** Apply cases to novel factual scenarios under timed conditions.
