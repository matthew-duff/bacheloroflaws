# 120 Offence—contempt of Tribunal

(1) A person commits an offence if:
(a) the person engages in conduct; and
(b) the conduct obstructs or hinders the Tribunal or an authorised person in the performance of the functions of the Tribunal. Penalty: Imprisonment for 12 months or 60 penalty units, or both.
(2) A person commits an offence if:
(a) the person engages in conduct; and
(b) the conduct would, if the Tribunal were a court of record, constitute a contempt of that court. Penalty: Imprisonment for 12 months or 60 penalty units, or both.
