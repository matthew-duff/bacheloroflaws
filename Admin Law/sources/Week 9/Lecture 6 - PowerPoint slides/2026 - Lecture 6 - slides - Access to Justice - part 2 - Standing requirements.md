[Obiter suggesting less restrictive approach to standing to seek equitable relief

Standing under the ADJR Act and the JR Act

24

Standing is often not contentious

See, for example, Broadbridge v Stammers (1987) 16 FCR 296, 298, per Bowen CJ, Beaumont and Gummow JJ:

[The case involved the interest of a post office employee who was seeking judicial review, relying on the ADJR Act, of a decision to close the post-office in which he worked.  His employment was secure.  He would, however, lose his accommodation above the post office

The Full Court quoted an earlier decision of Gummow J:

“... there flows from the decision ... a danger and peril to the interests of the applicant that is clear and imminent rather than remote, indirect or fanciful, and the applicant has an interest in the matter of an intensity and degree well above that of an ordinary member of the public”]
