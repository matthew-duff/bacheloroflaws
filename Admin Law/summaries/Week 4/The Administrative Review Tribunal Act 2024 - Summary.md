# The Administrative Review Tribunal Act 2024 (Cth)

## Overview
The ART Act 2024 replaced the Administrative Appeals Tribunal Act 1975 (Cth) and established the **Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)** as the successor to the AAT. The Act was enacted in response to concerns about the AAT's integrity, particularly regarding politically influenced appointments and declining decision quality.

## Key Changes from the AAT Act

### Transparent Appointments (s 209)
- The Minister must establish an **assessment panel** to assess whether candidates are suitable for appointment — addressing the concerns around "political stacking" of the AAT

### Merits Review Function
- The ART retains the core merits review function — determining the **"correct or preferable decision"** on the material before it
- The ART "stands in the shoes" of the original decision-maker, exercising the same powers and discretions

### Structure
- The ART operates with multiple tiers of review in some areas (e.g., three tiers for social security matters)
- A **guidance and appeals panel** may be established for further review within the ART

### Procedure
- Proceedings are to be conducted with **informality** and the ART is **not bound by the rules of evidence**
- Public hearings are presumed (cf. s 69 ART Act, compare s 35 AAT Act)
- Procedural fairness obligations apply

### Grounds for Removal (s 221)
- Clear statutory grounds for removal of members, including serious misconduct, incapacity, bankruptcy, or incompatible outside employment
- Termination by the Governor-General on ministerial recommendation
