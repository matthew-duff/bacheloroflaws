# Re Drake and Minister for Immigration and Ethnic Affairs (No. 2) (1979) 2 ALD 634

## Overview
The AAT rehearing of Mr Drake's deportation case following the Federal Court's remittal in Drake No. 1. Brennan J (sitting as AAT President) articulated the leading principles on the role of government policy in merits review.

## Key Issues
1. How the AAT should treat government policy when conducting merits review
2. The proper application of the "correct or preferable decision" test in practice
3. Whether Drake should be deported under the criminal deportation policy

## Key Holdings

### Role of Government Policy (Brennan J)
- The AAT should seek the **"correct or preferable decision on the material before it"** (adopting the Drake No. 1 formula)
- Rejected Smithers J's "standards of good government" test as being intended only to reflect the content of the deportation policy, not a general test
- Government policy is a **relevant consideration** that the AAT must weigh, but never a binding directive

### Departures from Government Policy
- Departures from lawful government policy should be **"cautious and sparing"**
- The AAT should only depart from valid ministerial policy where there are **"cogent reasons to the contrary"**, such as **"injustice in a particular case"**
- "Injustice" means a disproportionate detriment to the individual compared to the benefit to the community

### Principles Governing Policy
- A policy must be **intra vires** (within power)
- A policy must not be applied **inflexibly** — there must be willingness to hear arguments for non-application in individual cases
- Any discretionary power must be exercised **on the merits** of the particular case

### Outcome
- Brennan J decided that deportation was the correct decision; Drake's criminal conduct (drug trafficking) warranted deportation notwithstanding personal hardship

## Significance
- Established the enduring framework for how tribunals balance **consistency** (following policy) with **individual justice** (departing from policy in exceptional cases)
- The "cautious and sparing" approach to policy departure remains a guiding principle
- Complemented Drake No. 1 by giving practical content to the "correct or preferable decision" formula
