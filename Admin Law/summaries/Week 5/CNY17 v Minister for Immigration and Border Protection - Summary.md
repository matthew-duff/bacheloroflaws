# CNY17 v Minister for Immigration and Border Protection

## Citation
*CNY17 v Minister for Immigration and Border Protection* [2019] HCA 50; (2019) 268 CLR 76.

## Facts
CNY17 was a fast track applicant for a protection visa. After a delegate refused the visa, the matter went to the Immigration Assessment Authority (`IAA`) for limited review under Pt 7AA of the *Migration Act 1958* (Cth). The Secretary gave the IAA review material that included not only information about charges and convictions disclosed by CNY17, but also undisclosed departmental material suggesting aggressive behaviour, involvement in incidents or investigations, interest from "Det Intel", and prior refusal of bridging visas.

Under Pt 7AA, the IAA had to review the decision by considering the material given by the Secretary, generally without seeking new information and without interviewing the applicant. The IAA affirmed the refusal and said it had regard to the referred material, but its reasons did not mention the prejudicial material.

## Issues
1. Whether giving the IAA irrelevant and prejudicial material created apprehended bias.
2. Whether the Secretary failed to comply with s 473CB(1)(c) by giving material that could not reasonably be considered relevant to the review.

## Held
1. Yes, by majority (Nettle, Gordon and Edelman JJ; Kiefel CJ and Gageler J dissenting), a fair-minded lay observer might reasonably apprehend that the IAA might not bring an impartial mind to the review because it was required to consider irrelevant and prejudicial material.
2. Yes, by a different majority (Kiefel CJ, Gageler and Edelman JJ), the Secretary failed to comply with s 473CB(1)(c) by providing material that could not reasonably be considered relevant to the review. Nettle and Gordon JJ did not decide this point.
3. The Full Federal Court decision was reversed.

## Reasoning / Ratio
- The Court applied the *Ebner* approach to apprehended bias: identify the matter said to divert decision-making from the merits, then explain the logical connection between that matter and the feared departure from impartiality.
- Here, the impugned matter was the IAA's mandatory consideration of prejudicial material that was irrelevant to the criteria for protection and that painted CNY17 as a person of bad character or as otherwise unworthy of a visa.
- The logical connection was that such material could consciously or subconsciously influence the IAA's assessment of the applicant's credibility, character, or overall worthiness, even though those matters were not part of the legal task.
- The statutory setting mattered. The Secretary selected the material as "relevant"; the IAA was obliged to consider it; the applicant did not know about it and generally had no opportunity to respond; and the IAA's review process was limited and largely on the papers. Those features heightened the appearance that the review might be infected by prejudicial material.
- The majority treated the appearance of impartiality as critical. Even a professional decision-maker is not assumed to be immune from subconscious influence.
- On s 473CB(1)(c), the Court held that the Secretary's duty extends only to material that can reasonably be considered relevant to the review. Material incapable of rationally affecting the assessment of facts material to the review falls outside that duty.

## Significance
- Strong High Court authority that irrelevant and prejudicial information can found apprehended bias in administrative decision-making, especially where the tribunal or reviewer is required to consider the material.
- Important for migration review under Pt 7AA because the statutory scheme limited the applicant's ability to know of, or answer, prejudicial material.
- Shows the overlap between procedural fairness concerns and statutory limits on what material may lawfully be placed before a reviewer.

## Exam Use
- Cite it when arguing apprehended bias from exposure of a decision-maker to adverse material that is irrelevant to the statutory task.
- Use it with *Ebner*, *Isbester* and *Webb* to show that subconscious influence and the appearance of impartiality are enough; actual bias need not be proved.
- Use the statutory point if asked whether a repository of departmental material can simply be forwarded wholesale to a reviewer: `CNY17` says no, not if the material could not reasonably be considered relevant.
