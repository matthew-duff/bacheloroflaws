# Reference
Anthony Cassimatis, *LAWS3701 Administrative Law: Topic One - Law and Government: Introducing Administrative Law, Government Accountability and Administrative Justice* (Study Guide and Workshop Questions, 2026).

# Overview
This study guide introduces the foundational concepts of administrative law, focusing on the regulation of public powers and the relationship between the state and individuals. It outlines the core values, institutions, and mechanisms that ensure government accountability and administrative justice in Australia.

### Key Points:
- **Definition & Scope**: Administrative law regulates the exercise of public powers (statutory and non-statutory) by the executive branch. It is a subset of public law, closely linked to constitutional and human rights law.
- **Purpose**: To promote effective administration, ensure accountability, and check unlawful or unjust government action.
- **Core Values**: Legality, fairness, reasonableness/rationality, integrity, openness (transparency), efficiency, and effectiveness.
- **Executive Branch Components**: Includes the Monarch's representatives, PM/Premiers, Ministers, Cabinet, departmental officials, and statutory authorities (e.g., ART, ASIO, Ombudsman).
- **Public Powers**:
    - **Statutory**: Conferred by legislation (the "bread and butter" of admin law).
    - **Non-Statutory**: Prerogative powers, nationhood powers, and common law capacities (e.g., contracting).
- **Accountability Mechanisms**:
    - **Judicial Review**: Courts (HCA, Federal Court, Supreme Courts) checking legality.
    - **Merits Review**: Independent tribunals (ART, QCAT) determining the "correct or preferable" decision.
    - **Integrity Bodies**: Ombudsman, Human Rights Commissions, and anti-corruption watchdogs.
- **Three Overlapping Concepts**:
    - **Administrative Justice**: Respecting rights through transparent and timely processes.
    - **Government Accountability**: Legal, financial, and political responsibility.
    - **Good Administration**: Maintaining accurate records, avoiding delays, and professional development.

# Significance
This guide provides the pedagogical framework for the course, defining the key terms and institutions that students will encounter. It establishes the theoretical justification for administrative law (the rule of law and separation of powers) and introduces the practical application of these principles through workshop scenarios.
