[[Walker, The role of statutory interpretation in administrative law (2023) 109 AIAL Forum 33]]

# Reference
The Hon Justice Kristen Walker, 'The role of statutory interpretation in administrative law' (2023) 109 *AIAL Forum* 33.

# Overview / Key Points
This article explores the convergence of administrative law and statutory interpretation in Australia, arguing that statutory construction has become the dominant mode of reasoning in judicial review.

*   **Convergence of Judicial Review and Statutory Interpretation**: Walker J notes that judicial review of executive action is now primarily understood as enforcing the limits of statutory power.
*   **Common Law vs. Statute**: While Brennan J's view (that the source of review is the legislature's intention) has become dominant, Gageler J suggests a "draw" where common law techniques are used to discern that intention.
*   **Key Principles of Interpretation**:
    *   **Text, Context, and Purpose**: The starting and ending point is the statutory text, considered in light of its context and purpose.
    *   **Anthony Hordern Principle**: A general power cannot be used to circumvent limitations placed on a specific power covering the same subject matter (e.g., *Plaintiff M70*).
    *   **Implied Standards**: Standards of reasonableness, rationality, and procedural fairness are presumed to be intended by Parliament unless clearly displaced.
    *   **Materiality**: An error is only jurisdictional if it is "material"—meaning there was a realistic possibility that the decision could have been different but for the error (e.g., *SZMTA*, *MZAPC*, *Nathanson*).
*   **Non-Statutory Action**: Review of non-statutory (prerogative or executive) power is more complex but still grounded in the constitutional separation of powers and the rule of law.

# Significance
The article highlights that the "statutory turn" in Australian administrative law reinforces the democratic legitimacy of judicial review by framing it as the enforcement of the will of a democratically elected Parliament. It provides a contemporary judicial perspective on how core administrative law doctrines (like reasonableness and procedural fairness) are integrated into the framework of statutory construction.
