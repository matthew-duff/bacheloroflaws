# Law & Tech: Study Strategy and Notes Protocol

## 🎯 Objectives
This document outlines the strategy for processing materials, taking effective notes, and preparing for the two main assessments in Law & Tech:
1. **Written Assignment / Essay (40%)**
2. **End-of-semester Examination (60%)**

---

## 📂 Directory Structure & Material Utilization
To keep everything organized, we should establish a clear directory structure:

*   `/sources/` - Store all raw PDFs, cases, reading materials, and tech articles here.
*   `/summaries/` - Contains your parsed, finalized notes for each topic/week.
*   `/scaffolds/` - Contains test structures for potential exam problem questions.
*   `/essay/` - Contains materials, arguments, and drafts specifically for the written assignment.

### How to Utilize Materials
1.  **Interdisciplinary Readings**: Law & Tech bridges legal theory with computer science/technology. Understand the technical concepts (e.g., how a blockchain actually works, or how an LLM is trained) before applying the law.
2.  **Emerging Case Law & Legislation**: Focus on how existing legal frameworks (tort, contract, privacy) are being stretched to accommodate new tech, and look at new specific legislation (e.g., AI Acts, data protection laws).
3.  **Policy Papers**: Use whitepapers from governments or tech organizations to inform your essay arguments.

---

## 📝 Note-Taking Strategy

### The "Tech-Law Bridge" Note System

1.  **Pass 1: Concept & Rule Summaries**
    *   Create a markdown file for each topic/week.
    *   *Format:* Always have a section defining the *Technology* clearly in plain English, followed by the *Legal Framework* governing it.

2.  **Pass 2: The Essay "Idea Bank"**
    *   Keep a running list of specific regulatory gaps or overreaches you notice during lectures.
    *   *Format:* Tag these ideas so you can easily pull them into an essay outline later.

3.  **Pass 3: Exam Scaffolds**
    *   Synthesize the rules into "scaffolds" for problem questions.
    *   *Format:* Example - "Step 1: Is this data 'personal information' under the Privacy Act? -> Step 2: Was there a breach of an APP? -> Step 3: Remedies/Penalties."

---

## ✍️ Preparation for Written Assignment / Essay (40%)
This requires demonstrating a nuanced understanding of both the technology and the law.

*   **Focus Areas:** Regulatory challenges, comparative law (how different jurisdictions handle the tech), and proposing legal solutions.
*   **Preparation Method:**
    *   **Technical Accuracy:** Ensure your understanding of the tech is flawless. As a CS student, you have an edge here—use it to build highly technically competent arguments.
    *   **Start Early:** Due April 16. The hardest part is often narrowing down a broad tech topic into a specific, answerable legal question.

---

## ✍️ Preparation for End-of-semester Examination (60%)
The exam will likely test your ability to apply established legal principles to novel technological scenarios.

*   **Focus Areas:** Issue spotting across multiple domains (e.g., a scenario involving both privacy breaches and copyright infringement via an AI tool).
*   **Preparation Method:**
    *   **Flexible Scaffolds:** Build scaffolds that cover the elements of causes of action, but remain adaptable to weird, futuristic facts.
    *   **Analogical Reasoning:** Practice making analogies between older case law and new tech (e.g., is a smart contract just a vending machine?).
