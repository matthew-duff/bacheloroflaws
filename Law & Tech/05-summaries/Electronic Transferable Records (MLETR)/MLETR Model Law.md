# UNCITRAL Model Law on Electronic Transferable Records (MLETR)

**Reference:** UNCITRAL, *Model Law on Electronic Transferable Records with Explanatory Note* (United Nations, 2018).

## Overview
The UNCITRAL Model Law on Electronic Transferable Records (MLETR) aims to enable the legal use of electronic transferable records (ETRs) that are functionally equivalent to paper-based transferable documents or instruments (e.g., bills of lading, bills of exchange, promissory notes, warehouse receipts). It builds on the principles of **non-discrimination**, **technological neutrality**, and **functional equivalence**.

### Key Provisions
- **Scope (Art 1):** Applies to electronic records that are functional equivalents of paper-based transferable documents. It does not affect substantive law or consumer protection rules.
- **Functional Equivalence (Chapter II):**
    - **Writing (Art 8):** Met if information is accessible for subsequent reference.
    - **Signature (Art 9):** Met if a reliable method identifies the person and indicates their intention.
    - **Transferable Documents (Art 10):** An electronic record meets the requirement of a paper document if it contains the required information and uses a **reliable method** to:
        1. Identify the record as *the* ETR (**Singularity**).
        2. Render it capable of being subject to **control** from creation to expiry.
        3. Retain its **integrity** (unaltered state).
- **Control (Art 11):** The functional equivalent of "possession." Established if a reliable method identifies the person in exclusive control and allows for the transfer of that control.
- **General Reliability Standard (Art 12):** Provides an objective but flexible standard for assessing the "reliability" of methods used for signatures, control, and integrity, considering factors like operational rules, hardware/software security, and industry standards.
- **Change of Medium (Arts 17–18):** Allows for the replacement of a paper document with an ETR (and vice versa) provided a reliable method is used and the original is made inoperative.
- **Cross-Border Recognition (Art 19):** Prohibits discrimination against ETRs solely because they were issued or used abroad.

## Significance
The MLETR is a critical instrument for the "dematerialization" of trade finance and logistics. By providing a legal framework for "control" (as a digital proxy for physical possession), it overcomes the "uniqueness" challenge of digital files. This allows for fully paperless trade environments, facilitating faster, more secure, and more efficient international commerce. It is particularly significant for the implementation of the **Rotterdam Rules** and the modernization of supply chain management.
