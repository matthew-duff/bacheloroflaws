## Overview
The *Online Safety Act 2021* (Cth) established the eSafety Commissioner as an independent regulator to safeguard Australians from online harms. The Act provides a comprehensive framework for addressing cyber-bullying of children, adult cyber-abuse, non-consensual sharing of intimate images (image-based abuse), and illegal or restricted online content.

### Key Components
- **eSafety Commissioner:** Empowered to promote online safety, coordinate government activities, and administer various complaints and removal systems.
- **Removal Notices:** The Commissioner can issue notices to social media services, electronic services, and hosting providers to remove harmful material (cyber-bullying, cyber-abuse, intimate images, etc.).
- **Basic Online Safety Expectations (BOSE):** A set of standards for online service providers to ensure platform safety, driving transparency and accountability.
- **Industry Codes and Standards:** Enforceable rules for different sectors of the online industry (e.g., social media, app stores, search engines) to address harmful content (Classes 1A, 1B, 1C, and 2).
- **Age Restrictions:** Recent amendments (2024-2025) introduce mandatory age verification and social media bans for children under 16 to prevent exposure to age-inappropriate material (e.g., pornography, violent content).
- **Safety by Design:** An initiative encouraging services to embed safety features into their technology from the outset.
- **Enforcement:** Includes formal warnings, infringement notices, enforceable undertakings, and significant civil penalties (up to $49.5 million).

## Significance
The *Online Safety Act* represents a global first in creating a dedicated independent online safety regulator. It shifts the regulatory burden from individual users to online service providers, imposing a "Digital Duty of Care" (as recommended in the 2024 review). The Act is significant for its proactive approach to systemic change through the BOSE and industry-specific codes, particularly in its evolving response to emerging threats like harmful AI chatbots and the protection of minors through strict age-verification requirements.
