# Inaccessible Sources in Corporate Law Materials

Based on a review of your Corporate Law (LAWS3702) course materials, including the Learning Guide and Lecture Slides, the following reading resources are notably **inaccessible** or require specific action to access:

## 1. Primary Textbook: "Bottomley" (2025)
* **Title:** *Contemporary Australian Corporate Law* (3rd edn, Cambridge University Press, 2025) by S. Bottomley et al.
* **Why you don't have access:** According to the **Course Learning Guide** (p. 2) and **Lecture 0 Course Admin Slides**, a library subscription for this 2025 edition is "currently being negotiated by the Library with the publisher," meaning you do not have digital access through the university library system right now.
* **Alternative provided:** The teaching team has set alternative readings from last year's textbook: Jason Harris and Timothy Peters, *Company Law – Theories, Principles and Application* (3rd ed, LexisNexis, 2023) to cover possible shortages. You will see both "Bottomley OR Harris" listed in your weekly reading guides.
* **Action:** You have a "CUP Bottomley Discount Flyer" in your `Course Resources` if you wish to purchase it, otherwise, you should rely on the *Harris* alternative.

## 2. The Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)
* **Title:** *Corporations Act 2001* (Cth)
* **Why you don't have access:** The Act is not provided as a downloadable document in your course materials. The **Learning Guide** explicitly notes: *"A question we get every year is how to access the CA."* 
* **Alternative provided:** The guide suggests that you either consider buying physical extracts/consolidations, or use the free digital version on the Federal Register of Legislation and copy-paste key sections into your own notes.

## 3. General Case Law & Further Readings
* **Note:** Your `sources` directory currently contains only reading lists (markdown equivalents of Blackboard outlines) and lecture slides. It **does not contain any PDF downloads** of the underlying required cases (e.g., *Salomon v Salomon*, *Lee's Air Farming*) or journal articles. You will ostensibly have to access these individually via standard legal databases (LexisAdvance, Westlaw, Jade, or the library catalogue) each week as they have not been provided directly in the material dumps.
