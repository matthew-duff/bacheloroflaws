# Corporate Law: Study Strategy and Notes Protocol

## 🎯 Objectives
This document outlines the strategy for processing materials, taking effective notes, and preparing for the two main assessments in Corporate Law:
1. **Interactive Oral Assessment**
2. **End of Semester Exam**

---

## 📂 Directory Structure & Material Utilization
To keep everything organized, we should establish a clear directory structure:

*   `/sources/` - (Existing) Store all raw PDFs, lecture slides, reading materials, and raw case downloads here.
*   `/summaries/` - (To be created) Contains your parsed, finalized notes for each topic/week.
*   `/scaffolds/` - (To be created) Contains step-by-step problem-solving guides (IRAC/MIRAC) for the final exam.

### How to Utilize Materials
1.  **Legislation (Corporations Act 2001)**: Corporate Law is heavily statute-based. Always start with the relevant sections of the *Corporations Act*. Note down the specific section numbers and elements.
2.  **Case Law**: Summarize cases briefly. Focus on the *ratio decidendi* (the rule of law) and how it interprets the statute. 
3.  **Lectures & Textbooks**: Use these to understand the broader narrative and policy reasons behind the laws, which is vital for the oral assessment.

---

## 📝 Note-Taking Strategy

### The "Three-Pass" Note System
Instead of writing one massive document, structure notes iteratively to serve both assessments:

1.  **Pass 1: Weekly Summaries (The Foundation)**
    *   Create a markdown file for each topic/week (e.g., `Week 1 - Separate Legal Entity.md`).
    *   Synthesize lecture notes, textbook readings, and case summaries.
    *   *Format:* Use headings, bullet points, and highlight key statutory provisions and case names (e.g., using bold or specific tags).

2.  **Pass 2: Oral Assessment Prep (The "Explain it to me" Notes)**
    *   Extract key concepts and policy debates from the Weekly Summaries.
    *   Create conversational, flashcard-style Q&A documents.
    *   *Format:* Focus on "Why does this rule exist?", "What are the competing interests?", and explaining concepts in plain English.

3.  **Pass 3: Exam Scaffolds (The Final Exam Weapon)**
    *   Synthesize the Weekly Summaries into highly structured, actionable "scaffolds" for problem questions.
    *   *Format:* Use step-by-step MIRAC (Material Facts, Issue, Rule, Application, Conclusion) frameworks. E.g., "Step 1: Is there a breach of s 181? -> Check elements -> Cite case -> Apply facts."

---

## 🗣️ Preparation for Interactive Oral Assessment
Oral assessments test your fundamental understanding, your ability to articulate legal principles clearly, and your capacity to think on your feet.

*   **Focus Areas:** Policy rationale, overarching themes, and the "why" behind the law (e.g., piercing the corporate veil, agency costs).
*   **Preparation Method:**
    *   **Mock Interviews:** Practice explaining concepts out loud without looking at your notes. 
    *   **Concept Mapping:** Map out how different areas of Corporate Law interact.
    *   **Prepare for Follow-ups:** Be ready to discuss the implications of shifting case law or hypothetical changes to the facts.

---

## ✍️ Preparation for End of Semester Exam
Exams in Corporate Law are typically problem-based and time-pressured.

*   **Focus Areas:** Issue spotting, strict statutory interpretation, application of the law to the facts, and clear structure.
*   **Preparation Method:**
    *   **Scaffolds:** Build very tight, efficient scaffolds. Make them easy to navigate during a time crunch.
    *   **Practice Exams:** Do past papers under timed conditions to practice identifying multiple overlapping issues (e.g., a director breaching multiple duties simultaneously).
    *   **Statute Mastery:** Ensure you can navigate the *Corporations Act* rapidly. Know exactly where the director penalty provisions, insolvent trading defenses, and oppression remedies live.
