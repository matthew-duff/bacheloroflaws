Problem 1, Part 2: Transcript of Fictional Zoom Meeting

[Fictional Zoom meeting starts]

_Morgan (Partner):_

Hi Riley, how are things going over at Evergreen?

_Riley (GC):_

Busy, as always. Thanks for making the time. We’re dealing with two linked discussions internally.

First, the angel investor who’s considering a sizeable investment wants us to adopt a ‘public-benefit

purpose statement.’ Second, some people here are starting to question whether our current set-up is

the right structure as we scale.

_Morgan (Partner):_

Right. You’re still operating as an unlisted public company?

_Riley (GC):_

Yes. It’s worked well and helped when we brought in our 40-odd sophisticated investors early on, but

as we’re growing, there’s a bit of debate about whether it remains the best fit.

_Morgan (Partner):_

Let’s talk about the purpose statement first. What’s being proposed?

_Riley (GC):_

Something like ‘advancing sustainable agriculture’ and supporting ‘community-based growing

projects.’ One founder is very enthusiastic, another is worried it’ll narrow the company’s mandate or

create expectations we can’t realistically meet. Marketing likes it because the seed-bank trial with the

university aligns nicely with the message.

_Morgan (Partner):_

Understood. Statements like that do sit in the governance and reputational space. They tend to shape

how stakeholders interpret decisions.

_Riley (GC):_

Yep, and that’s already becoming apparent. People are reading a lot into what the statement might

imply.

_Morgan (Partner):_

Alright. So, you need to know its significance, especially whether it generates any legal constraints.

_Riley (GC):_

Yes. The Board can see the signaling effects of a document like, but doesn’t understand the legals.

_Morgan (Partner):_

Now, you mentioned people are talking about converting to a proprietary company.

_Riley (GC):_

Yes, mostly marketing and a couple of founders. They think it might simplify reporting ‘while things

stabilise.’ I’m not sure they’ve thought about the consequences.

_Morgan (Partner):_

Yes, it would be a big change for the company and there are procedural hurdles._Riley (GC):_

Exactly. And that’s where I want the focus. The Board should know the process exists and what it

involves, but the emphasis needs to stay on the practical and strategic implications.

_Riley (GC):_

If you can prepare a short note addressing just these two points (the purpose statement and the

implications of changing company type) that would be perfect. High-level only, with the procedural

steps noted but not dominating the discussion.

_Morgan (Partner):_

Absolutely. I’ll draft something and send it through this week.

_Riley (GC):_

Thanks, Morgan. Appreciate it.

[call ends]
