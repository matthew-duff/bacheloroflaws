## 🎯 PART A: CORE CONCEPTS & STATUTORY FRAMEWORK

### 1. Key Terminology & Distinctions
*   **Company:** An artificial legal person created by registration under the *Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)* (**CA**).
*   **Corporation (s 57A):** A broader term including companies, body corporates, unincorporated bodies able to sue/hold property, and ATSI corporations. Exceptions: exempt public authorities and corporations sole.
*   **Separate Legal Personality (SEP) (s 119):** A company comes into existence as a separate legal entity upon registration.
*   **Insiders vs. Outsiders:** Corporate law governs relations between *insiders* (members, directors) and *outsiders* (creditors, regulators, contracting parties). It does not directly cover the company's role as an employer, taxpayer, or environmental actor.

### 2. The Registration Process (ss 117-119)
1.  **Application (s 117):** Prescribed info (name, members, directors, address, shares/guarantee) lodged with ASIC.
2.  **ASIC Role (s 118):** ASIC reviews, issues ACN, registers the company, and issues a certificate of registration.
3.  **Conclusive Evidence (s 1274(7A)):** Certificate of registration is conclusive evidence of compliance with requirements (though ASIC can challenge it).
4.  **Creation (s 119):** Company legally exists at the beginning of the registration day.

### 3. Classifying Companies
*   **By Member Liability (s 112):**
    *   **Limited by Shares:** Members liable only for unpaid share amounts. Must use "Ltd" or "Pty Ltd" (s 148).
    *   **Limited by Guarantee:** No share capital. Members guarantee a fixed sum on winding up. Must be public. Used for non-profits.
    *   **Unlimited Liability:** Members fully liable on winding up. Used by professional practices (e.g., to easily return capital).
    *   **No Liability (NL):** Public companies solely for mining purposes. No contractual right to recover unpaid calls on shares; shares are simply forfeited.
*   **Proprietary (Pty) vs. Public:**
    *   **Proprietary (Pty Ltd):** Share capital required, min 1 / max 50 non-employee shareholders (s 113), min 1 resident director (s 201A(1)), cannot engage in public fundraising (Ch 6D).
    *   **Large vs. Small Pty (s 45A / Regs 1.0.02B):** Must meet 2 of 3 to be "Large": Revenue > $50m, Assets > $25m, Employees > 100. Large companies have strict financial reporting and auditing obligations.
    *   **Public (Ltd/NL):** Residual category (s 112). Min 1 shareholder (no max), min 3 directors (2 resident) + 1 secretary, can public fundraise.

### 4. Regulatory Framework & ASIC
*   **Constitutional Basis:** Relies on the **referral of powers** from States to the Cth (s 51(xxxvii)) following the invalidation of earlier schemes (*Huddart Parker*, *NSW v Commonwealth*, *Re Wakim*, *R v Hughes*).
*   **ASIC's Role (ASIC Act s 1(2)):** Conflicting mandate to both facilitate commercial efficiency (reduce costs) AND enforce the law/protect investors.
*   **Civil Penalty Regime (Pt 9.4B):** Pyramidal enforcement model. Criminal → Civil penalties → Compensation → Infringement notices → Regulatory guidance.
    *   **s 1317E:** Lists civil penalty provisions (e.g., directors' duties ss 180-183). ASIC seeks a **declaration of contravention**, then can seek pecuniary penalties (s 1317G), relinquishment (s 1317GAB), or disqualification (s 206C). Compensation (s 1317H) does not require a prior declaration.

## 🗣️ PART B: ORAL ASSESSMENT PREP (THE "WHY")
*Use these flashcard-style Q&As to practice explaining concepts and policy rationales out loud.*

**Q: What are the "Four Perennial Tensions" in corporate law?**
*Answer Checklist:*
1.  **Group vs. Individual:** Should law recognize the corporation, or just the individuals within it? (Limited liability vs. personal responsibility).
2.  **Management vs. Ownership:** How do we handle the separation of control (directors) from investment (shareholders)? (The Berle & Means agency cost problem).
3.  **Facilitation vs. Intervention:** Should corporate law be a hands-off set of "off-the-rack" rules (laissez-faire) or impose mandatory standards to protect the public?
4.  **Public vs. Private:** Are companies purely private wealth-generating vehicles, or do they serve a broader public purpose with social responsibilities?

**Q: Explain the historical evolution from the "Bubble Act" to the modern corporation.**
*Answer Checklist:*
*   **18th Century:** Royal charters granted monopoly privileges (public purpose). The South Sea Bubble burst (1720), leading to the Bubble Act which banned unincorporated joint-stock companies.
*   **Workaround:** Lawyers used deeds of settlement (trusts) to create private, transferrable-share businesses in equity (shifting from public to private purpose).
*   **1844 Joint Stock Companies Act:** Introduced incorporation by routine *registration* (democratizing the corporation) and transparency (mandatory accounting), but no limited liability.
*   **1855/1856 Acts:** Added limited liability as an entitlement and championed freedom of contract (laissez-faire).
*   **Salomon (1897):** Solidified that the law recognized the company as distinct from its members, paving the way for the one-person proprietary company (even though that wasn't the original legislative intent).

**Q: Why is the Australian Corporations Act constitutionally brittle?**
*Answer Checklist:*
*   Historically, under s 51(xx), the High Court ruled the Cth could only regulate *existing* corporations, not *incorporate* new ones (*Huddart Parker* (1909), affirmed in *NSW v Cth* (1990)).
*   Co-operative schemes of the 1980s failed due to 9 different bureaucracies.
*   Cross-vesting laws were struck down in *Re Wakim* and *R v Hughes*.
*   Today's *Corporations Act 2001* relies entirely on the States successfully **referring their powers** to the Cth under s 51(xxxvii). If the political agreement ends, the national system collapses.

**Q: What were the key takeaways from the Banking Royal Commission regarding enforcement?**
*Answer Checklist:*
*   It exposed severe corporate misconduct driven by perverse incentives and conflicts of interest.
*   **Criticism of ASIC:** ASIC was overly reliant on the bottom of the enforcement pyramid (negotiated outcomes, enforceable undertakings, infringement notices) instead of seeking declarations of contravention or criminal penalties for massive corporate entities. The law was not fulfilling its deterrent function. "Entities not held to account."

## ✍️ PART C: EXAM SCAFFOLDS (ISSUE SPOTTING & MIRAC)

### Scaffold 1: Is [Entity] a validly formed company under the CA?
*Use this if facts involve pre-registration activities, promoters, or a challenged incorporation.*
1.  **Issue:** Has [Company] been legally registered and what are the consequences?
2.  **Rule/Statute:** A company is registered by lodging an application under **s 117**. It comes into existence as a body corporate on the day of registration (**s 119**).
3.  **Analysis/Application:**
    *   Look for a certificate of registration. Under **s 1274(7A)**, this is conclusive evidence of compliance.
    *   If pre-registration: Does the company have capacity? No. Check **s 131** for pre-registration contracts (promoters are personally liable unless the company ratifies it post-registration).
4.  **Conclusion:** The company exists (or does not exist yet) and [Party] can/cannot sue it in its own name.

### Scaffold 2: Classifying the Company (Reporting & Governance Duties)
*Use this if the facts ask whether a company must lodge audited accounts or if they are in breach of director minimums.*
1.  **Issue:** Is [Company] a large or small proprietary company, or a public company, and what are its obligations?
2.  **Rule/Statute:**
    *   **Pty Ltd (s 113):** Max 50 non-employee members, must have share capital, no Ch 6D fundraising.
    *   **Large vs. Small (s 45A & Regs):** Test 2 out of 3: Revenue > $50m, Assets > $25m, Employees > 100.
3.  **Analysis/Application:**
    *   Apply the financials from the facts against the thresholds.
    *   If Large Pty or Public -> Mandatory financial reporting and audit obligations under **ss 292, 301**.
    *   If Small Pty -> Typically exempt unless directed by 5% of shareholders or ASIC (**ss 293-294**).
    *   Check minimum directors: Pty = 1 resident (**s 201A(1)**); Public = 3 (2 resident) (**s 201A(2)**).
4.  **Conclusion:** Determine classification and advise on compliance.

### Scaffold 3: Challenging/Enforcing Civil Penalties (ASIC Action)
*Use this if the facts state ASIC is investigating a director or company.*
1.  **Issue:** What enforcement pathway can ASIC take under Pt 9.4B?
2.  **Rule:**
    *   ASIC must apply for a declaration of contravention (**s 1317J(1)**) for designated civil penalty provisions (**s 1317E**).
    *   Once declared, ASIC can seek pecuniary penalties (**s 1317G**), relinquishment (**s 1317GAB**), or disqualification (**s 206C**).
    *   Compensation (**s 1317H**) can be sought by ASIC or the company without a prior declaration.
3.  **Application:** Identify the breached provision and match the possible penalty to the facts. Note *Royal Commission* policy critiques if evaluating ASIC's likely strategy (i.e., ASIC expected to litigate, not just settle).
4.  **Conclusion:** Advise on maximum exposures for the directors/company.
