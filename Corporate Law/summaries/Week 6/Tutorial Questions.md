# Tutorial 4: Building on Content from Lecture 5 (Corporate Contracting)

Grandview Property Developers Ltd (‘GPD’) is a property development company based in Noosa that focuses on purchasing old, dilapidated homes and then renovating them for sale. The directors of GPD are Ann, Brian and Carol, with Carol taking care of the finances of the company. GPD employs a team of around 10 business development managers to identify properties for purchase, and finances most of its purchases through loans from banks and property finance providers. 

David is a new business development manager with GPD and has identified a property in Maryborough for purchase by GPD. He contacts Quickee Property Lenders Pty Ltd (QPL) (one of the property financiers that GPD has dealt with previously) and speaks with Elizabeth (who is a lending manager). Elizabeth has only worked from QPL for a few months. David requests a loan of $200,000 and shows Elizabeth photos of the property. He enthusiastically explains how he believes it is very good value investment that will double in value. 

By email the next day, David requests Elizabeth to transfer $200,000 into an account named ‘GPD Maryborough’. David describes himself in his email signature block as ‘Business Development Director’. Elizabeth is somewhat confused about this arrangement. Upon checking QPL’s records, she notices that QPL has always previously advanced funds into an account named ‘GPD Ltd’. Elizabeth phones GPD’s office and the phone is answered by the receptionist named Fiona (unbeknownst to Elizabeth, Fiona is David’s second cousin). In response to Elizabeth’s questions, Fiona says that Carol told her to say that GPD is using a new accounting arrangement (which is untrue). 

Elizabeth then deposits the $200,000 requested by David into the ‘GPD Maryborough’ account. One month later, after the first repayment by was due but not paid, Elizabeth rings GPD’s office to follow up. Carol answers and explains that David and Fiona have left the company and moved overseas. Carol states that she knows nothing about the ‘GPD Maryborough’ account. 

Advise QPL whether GPD could be held liable for the $200,000 loan.
