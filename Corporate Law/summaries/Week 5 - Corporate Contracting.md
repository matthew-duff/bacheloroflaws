# Week 5 - Corporate Contracting

## Key Statutes
* **s 124 CA**: Legal capacity and powers of a company to contract.
* **s 126 CA**: Agent exercising a company’s power to make contracts without seal.
* **s 127(1) CA**: Execution of documents without common seal (2 directors, or 1 dir + 1 sec, or sole director/sec).
* **s 127(2) CA**: Execution of documents with common seal (witnessed as in 127(1)).
* **s 128 CA**: Entitlement to make assumptions.
* **s 129 CA**: The specific statutory assumptions (Internal rule, Implied authority, Apparent authority, Execution).

## 1. The Contracting Problem
*   Companies must manifest an intention to contract through human intermediaries.
*   **Three rules of attribution** (**_Meridian Global Funds_**):
    1.  Primary rules (Direct Contracting via Board/Delegates).
    2.  General rules (Indirect Contracting via Agency).
    3.  Special rules.

## 2. Direct Contracting (s 127)
*   **Method:** A company contracts directly if executed by someone with *original authority* (the Board) or via formal execution.
*   **Formal Execution:**
    1.  **Without Seal (s 127(1)):** Signed by 2 directors, or 1 director + 1 company secretary, or the sole director/secretary of a Pty company.
    2.  **With Seal (s 127(2)):** Seal is fixed and witnessed by the same combinations as above.
*   Requires both *substantive authority* (board resolution to enter contract) and *formal authority* (board resolution to execute documents).

## 3. Indirect Contracting (Agency)
*   Individuals act as agents for the company (principal) to bind it to a third party. Governed by **s 126** and general common law agency principles.
*   **Actual Authority:** Principle actually agreed the agent can act.
    *   **Express actual authority:** Granted via contract, board resolution, or constitution (**s 198A**).
    *   **Implied actual authority:**
        *   *Through position:* Implied by the role (e.g., CEO/MD implied to do things within usual scope - **_Entwells_**; **_Hely Hutchinson_**).
        *   *Through conduct:* Implied by acquiescence of the board (**_Brick & Pipe v Occidental Life_**).

## 4. Defects in Authority & Apparent Authority
*   What happens if the "agent" had no actual authority? We must protect innocent third parties ("Winners and Losers").
*   **Apparent (Ostensible) Authority:** Arises by estoppel, not actual grant of power.
    *   **Common Law Requirements:**
        1. A "holding out" (representation) that the agent had authority.
        2. Representation made by someone who *had actual authority*.
        3. Reliance on that representation by the third party.
    *   **Key Cases:**
        *   **_Freeman & Lockyer_**: Company bound because board allowed individual to act as MD, creating apparent authority.
        *   **_Crabtree-Vickers_**: Company NOT bound. The person holding out the agent didn't have actual authority themselves.

## 5. The Indoor Management Rule & Statutory Assumptions
*   **Internal Management Rule (Common Law):** Third parties can assume internal company rules were followed regularly ("Turquand's case").
    *   *Exception:* Doesn't apply if third party had *actual knowledge* of irregularity or was *put on inquiry* (suspicious circumstances like **_Northside Developments_**).
*   **Statutory Assumptions (ss 128 & 129):** Override/run parallel to common law.
    *   **s 128:** Third party can assert assumptions even if the agent acts fraudulently/forges docs, UNLESS they knew or suspected the assumption was incorrect.
    *   **s 129(1):** Constitution and Replaceable Rules complied with (Statutory Indoor Management).
    *   **s 129(2):** Person appearing as director/secretary on ASIC records is duly appointed and has customary authority.
    *   **s 129(3):** Person held out by company as officer/agent has customary authority.
    *   **s 129(5) & (6):** Document appearing executed under s 127 (with or without seal) is duly executed.
