# Case Summary: Gilford Motor Co Ltd v Horne [1933] Ch 935

**Court:** Court of Appeal (UK)
**Key Principle:** Piercing the Corporate Veil — The "Sham" or "Facade" Exception.

---

## 1. Facts
*   **The Covenant:** Mr. Horne was the Managing Director of Gilford Motor Co. His employment contract included a restrictive covenant (Clause 9) prohibiting him from soliciting the company's customers after leaving his position.
*   **The Breach:** After resigning, Horne set up his own business in competition with Gilford Motor Co.
*   **The Device:** To circumvent the restrictive covenant, Horne incorporated a company, **J M Horne & Co Ltd**. He was not a director or shareholder (his wife and an associate were), but he effectively controlled and ran the business.
*   **The Action:** Gilford Motor Co sought an injunction against both Horne and his new company to stop the solicitation of their customers.

## 2. Issues
1.  Was the restrictive covenant enforceable (or was it an unreasonable restraint of trade)?
2.  Could the court grant an injunction against the *company* (J M Horne & Co Ltd), which was not a party to the original contract between Gilford and Horne?

## 3. Ratio Decidendi
### The "Sham/Facade" Principle
The Court of Appeal held that the company was formed as a **"device," "stratagem," "cloak," or "sham"** specifically to mask Horne's breach of his contractual obligations. 

*   **Lord Hanworth MR:** "I am quite satisfied that this company was formed as a device, a stratagem, in order to mask the effective carrying on of a business of Mr EB Horne... under what is a cloak or a sham."
*   **Lawrence LJ:** The company was a "mere cloak or sham for the purpose of enabling the defendant to commit a breach of his covenant."

### Piercing the Veil
Because the company was used as a vehicle for fraud or to evade a legal obligation, the court "pierced the corporate veil." It treated the company's actions as the actions of Horne himself.

## 4. Legal Significance (Exam Focus)
*   **Exception to *Salomon*:** This is a primary authority for the principle that the separate legal personality of a company (established in *Salomon v Salomon & Co Ltd*) cannot be used to evade existing legal obligations or to practice a fraud.
*   **The "Evasion" Principle:** Later clarified in *Prest v Petrodel Resources Ltd* [2013], *Gilford Motor* is the classic example of the **Evasion Principle**: the court may disregard the corporate veil if a person is under an existing legal obligation/restriction which they deliberately evade by interposing a company under their control.
*   **Remedy:** The court granted an injunction against *both* the individual (Horne) and the company (J M Horne & Co Ltd), even though the company was not a party to the contract.

## 5. Quick Revision Table

| Feature | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Core Concept** | Evasion of legal obligations via a corporate "sham". |
| **Key Quote** | "A device, a stratagem... a cloak or a sham." |
| **Application** | Use when a defendant creates a company to do what they are personally forbidden from doing (e.g., breaching a non-compete). |
| **Relationship to *Salomon*** | Limits the protection of separate legal personality where it is used for "masking" improper conduct. |
