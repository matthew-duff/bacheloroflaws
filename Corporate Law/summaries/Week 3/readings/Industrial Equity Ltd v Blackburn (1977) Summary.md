# Case Summary: Industrial Equity Ltd v Blackburn (1977) 137 CLR 567

## 📌 Exam Overview
**Core Principle:** Separate Legal Personality in Corporate Groups.
**Key Takeaway:** A holding company cannot treat the profits of its subsidiaries as its own for the purpose of declaring dividends. Each company is a separate legal entity.

---

## ⚖️ Legal Significance: Separate Legal Personality
*   **Entity Isolation:** Confirms the *Salomon* principle applies strictly to corporate groups. The "group" is not a single legal person; it is a collection of separate entities.
*   **Creditor Protection:** Creditors of a subsidiary can only look to that subsidiary's assets. Conversely, the holding company's capacity to pay dividends is limited to its own realized profits, protecting its own creditors from capital depletion based on "paper" group profits.
*   **Accounting vs Law:** While accounting standards (and the *Companies Act*) require consolidated "group accounts" for transparency, these do not override the legal reality that profits belong to the specific company that earned them until formally distributed (e.g., via dividend) to the parent.

---

## 📝 Facts
*   **Industrial Equity Ltd (IEL)** was a holding company.
*   IEL declared a "special distribution" (dividend) to shareholders.
*   **The Problem:** The total dividend amount exceeded IEL's own accumulated profits but was well within the **consolidated group profits** (which included profits held by its subsidiaries).
*   **The Resolution:** The dividend was to be paid partly in cash and partly in shares of a subsidiary (*Minerva Centre Ltd*), with some shareholders receiving only cash (discriminatory distribution).

---

## 🔨 Ratio Decidendi
1.  **Source of Dividends:** Dividends can only be paid out of the profits of the **individual company** declaring them. "Group profits" disclosed in consolidated accounts are irrelevant for determining the legality of a dividend under s 376 (now see *Corporations Act* s 254T).
2.  **Timing of Profits:** Profits must exist in the company **at the time the dividend is declared**. A company cannot declare a dividend in anticipation of future profits or distributions from subsidiaries that have not yet occurred.
3.  **Equality of Distribution (Article Construction):** Unless specifically authorized by the articles (constitution), directors cannot discriminate by paying some shareholders in cash and others in specific assets (shares).

---

## 💡 Quick Revision Points
*   **Can a parent company use a subsidiary's cash to pay its own dividends?** No, not until the subsidiary formally declares and pays a dividend to the parent.
*   **Does the requirement for consolidated accounts merge the entities?** No. Consolidated accounts are for *information* only; they do not create a single legal "group" profit pool.
*   **Statutory Link:** Reflects the principle now found in **s 254T** of the *Corporations Act 2001* (though s 254T now uses a balance sheet test, the principle of entity separation remains fundamental).
