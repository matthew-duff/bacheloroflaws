# Exam Summary: Prest v Petrodel Resources Ltd [2013] UKSC 34

## 1. Case Overview
*   **Court:** UK Supreme Court
*   **Key Issue:** Whether the court could "pierce the corporate veil" to reach assets held by companies controlled by a husband in matrimonial proceedings.
*   **Outcome:** The appeal was allowed, but **not** by piercing the corporate veil. Instead, the court found the properties were held on **resulting trust** for the husband.

---

## 2. The "Concealment vs. Evasion" Principles
Lord Sumption clarified the limited circumstances under which the corporate veil can be pierced, distinguishing between two distinct principles:

### A. The Concealment Principle (Does NOT involve piercing)
*   **Definition:** Using a company to mask the real actors or facts.
*   **Legal Effect:** The court looks *behind* the veil to discover the true facts. It does not disregard the company's separate legal personality but simply identifies the true nature of the transaction or the identity of the real parties.
*   **Application:** Often involves agency or trust law (e.g., the company holds property as a nominee/trustee).

### B. The Evasion Principle (The ONLY basis for piercing)
*   **Definition:** Using a company's separate legal personality to **evade** an existing legal obligation, liability, or restriction, or to frustrate its enforcement.
*   **Legal Effect:** The court **pierces** the veil to deprive the company or its controller of the advantage they would otherwise obtain from the company's separate legal personality.
*   **Threshold:** This is a "residual" power of last resort. If another legal remedy (like equity or agency) exists, the veil should **not** be pierced.

---

## 3. Ratio Decidendi
1.  **Strict Limit on Piercing:** The corporate veil can only be pierced under the **Evasion Principle** where a person is under an existing legal obligation/restriction which they deliberately evade by interposing a company.
2.  **No "Family Law Exception":** There is no special, wider jurisdiction in matrimonial law (under s 24 of the *Matrimonial Causes Act 1973*) to pierce the veil. Family courts must apply the same general principles of company law as other courts.
3.  **Resulting Trust:** In this specific case, the properties were held by the companies on **resulting trust** for the husband because he had provided the purchase funds and the companies failed to provide evidence to the contrary. Thus, the husband was "entitled" to the property in equity.

---

## 4. Legal Significance (Exam Points)
*   **Reaffirmation of *Salomon*:** Strongly reaffirms the principle of separate legal personality.
*   **Narrowing of Exceptions:** Effectively narrowed the "piercing" doctrine to almost zero, making it a remedy of last resort.
*   **Impropriety Requirement:** Mere "impropriety" or "injustice" is insufficient to pierce the veil; there must be specific evasion of a pre-existing legal obligation.
*   **Trust Law as an Alternative:** Highlights that many "piercing" cases are actually better explained through trust or agency law (Concealment).
