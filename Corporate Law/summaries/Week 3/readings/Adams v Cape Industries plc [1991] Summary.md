# Case Summary: Adams v Cape Industries plc [1991] 1 All ER 929

| **Court** | UK Court of Appeal |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Key Topics** | Separate Legal Personality, Corporate Groups, Piercing the Veil, Agency, Jurisdiction |
| **Relevance** | Leading authority on the "Single Economic Unit" and "Facade/Sham" exceptions to *Salomon*. |

---

## 1. Facts
*   **Cape Industries plc** (UK parent) headed a group mining asbestos in South Africa. 
*   **NAAC** (wholly-owned US subsidiary) marketed the asbestos in Texas.
*   Employees in Texas sued Cape for asbestos-related injuries. Cape had no physical presence in Texas but NAAC did.
*   Default judgments were entered against Cape in the US. The plaintiffs sought to enforce these judgments in England.
*   **Issue:** Could the English court "pierce the veil" to treat Cape and its subsidiaries as one entity, or treat the subsidiary as an agent, to establish that Cape was "present" in the US jurisdiction?

---

## 2. Key Principles & Ratio Decidendi

The Court of Appeal identified three main arguments for piercing the corporate veil, rejecting all of them on the facts:

### A. The "Single Economic Unit" Argument (Rejected)
*   **Principle:** The court rejected the idea that a corporate group should be treated as a single entity simply because it functions as one economically.
*   **Ratio:** Each company in a group is a separate legal entity with separate rights and liabilities. The court will not ignore the separate legal personality of group members just because it would be "just" to do so.
*   **Distinguished:** *DHN Food Distributors v Tower Hamlets* (which suggested a single economic unit approach) was confined to its specific statutory context (compulsory land acquisition).

### B. The "Facade or Sham" Argument (Narrowly Accepted)
*   **Principle:** The veil can be pierced where a company is a "mere facade concealing the true facts."
*   **Ratio:** To be a facade, the company must be used for a **dishonest or improper purpose** (e.g., to evade an existing legal obligation or frustrate the law). 
*   **Application:** Cape’s restructuring of its group to avoid *future* (not existing) legal liabilities was held to be a **legitimate use of the corporate form**. It is not improper to use a subsidiary to insulate a parent from future liability.

### C. The "Agency" Argument (Rejected)
*   **Principle:** A subsidiary may be an agent for its parent, but this must be proven by evidence of an actual agency relationship.
*   **Ratio:** The mere fact of 100% ownership and control by the parent does not create an agency relationship. The subsidiary must be carrying on the *parent's* business, not its own.
*   **Application:** NAAC carried on its own business (even if directed by Cape), so no agency was found.

---

## 3. Legal Significance (Exam Focus)

*   **Strict Adherence to *Salomon*:** Reaffirmed that the principle of separate legal personality applies strictly to corporate groups.
*   **Limits on Judicial Discretion:** The court cannot pierce the veil simply because it is "fair" or "just" (overruling the broader approach in *Re A Company* [1985]).
*   **Legitimacy of Asset Protection:** Explicitly stated that using corporate structures to minimize future liability is a legitimate right of shareholders.
*   **Jurisdiction:** Established that for a foreign judgment to be enforceable, the defendant must be "present" in the foreign jurisdiction (either physically or through an agent), and a subsidiary's presence is not enough.

---

## 4. Quick Revision: The "Three Pillars" of Adams
1.  **Single Economic Unit:** No. Groups are separate entities.
2.  **Facade/Sham:** Only if used to evade *existing* legal obligations.
3.  **Agency:** Not presumed. Requires proof of an actual agency relationship.

---
> [!note] Note on Subsequent Law
> While *Adams* remains the leading case on the "single economic unit," the "facade" exception was further refined in **Prest v Petrodel Resources Ltd [2013]**, which distinguished between the "evasion principle" (piercing the veil) and the "concealment principle" (looking behind the veil).
