

## Key Statutes
* **s 9 CA**: Limited liability concept
* **s 46 CA**: Tests for a subsidiary
* **s 50 CA**: Related companies
* **s 50AA CA**: Controlled entities
* **s 114 CA**: Minimum 1 member; SEP applies to 'one person' companies
* **s 119 CA**: A company comes into existence on registration (Separate Legal Personality)
* **s 124 CA**: Legal capacity and powers of an individual/body corporate
* **s 125 CA**: Company may restrict its powers (objects clause)
* **s 187 CA**: Duties of directors of subsidiaries
* **s 588V-588X CA**: Holding company liability for insolvent trading

## 1. Separate Legal Personality (SEP)
*   A company is a **Separate Legal Entity (SEP)** from its members and managers.
*   **Key Case:** **_Salomon v Salomon & Co Ltd_ [1897]**
    *   **Rule:** A new legal person comes into being upon incorporation. Its rights and liabilities are separate from its members, even if incorporated and controlled by one person ("one person" companies).
    *   **Facts:** Mr. Salomon incorporated a company, took debentures as security. When the company failed, the liquidator argued the company was a sham. House of Lords held it was a separate entity, so Salomon's secured debt was valid.
*   **Consequences of SEP:**
    *   General: Legal capacity and limited liability.
    *   Specific:
        *   Separate legal duties and rights.
        *   Can contract with their controllers (**_Lee v Lee’s Air Farming Ltd_**).
        *   Ownership of property is separate from shareholders (**_Macaura v Northern Assurance Co Ltd_**).
        *   Perpetual succession.
        *   Separate tax obligations.

## 2. Corporate Capacity (ss 124, 125 CA)
*   **s 124:** A company has the legal capacity and powers of an individual, plus specific corporate powers (e.g., issue shares, grant debentures).
*   **s 125:** A company's constitution may contain an "objects clause" restricting or prohibiting the exercise of certain powers.

## 3. Limited Liability
*   Shareholders' liability for the company's debts is limited to the amount (if any) unpaid on their shares.
*   **Justifications:** Efficiency or vested interests? Protects investors and encourages entrepreneurial risk-taking.

## 4. Problems with SEP: Corporate Fault & Veil Piercing
*   **Corporate Fault / Attribution (How is a company liable for human acts?):**
    1.  **Directly:** Through 'alter egos' (e.g., **_Lennard’s Carrying Co Ltd_**, **_Tesco Supermarkets Ltd v Nattrass_**, **_Meridian Global Funds_**).
    2.  **Vicariously:** Through agents (**_Mousell Bros Ltd_**).
    3.  **Holistically:** Through defective corporate systems/cultures (e.g., **_Criminal Code Act 1995_ (Cth) Pt 2.5**).
*   **Exceptions to SEP (Lifting / Piercing the Veil):**
    1.  Fraud & sham companies (**_Re Darby_**; **_Adams v Cape Industries_**).
    2.  Companies used to avoid existing obligations (**_Gilford Motor Co v Horne_**; **_Jones v Lipman_**; **_Prest v Petrodel_**).
    3.  Subservient companies that are mere agents for controllers (**_Smith, Stone & Knight_**).
    4.  Statutory exceptions (e.g., **s 588G & V CA** for insolvent trading, tax/super obligations).
    5.  Tort creditors of underfunded subsidiaries? (**_Briggs v James Hardie_**).

## 5. Corporate Groups
*   A corporate group is NOT a single company, but a collection of companies related through ownership or control.
*   Generally, each company in the group is a separate legal entity (**_Walker v Wimborne_**). Courts respect the veil between parent and child companies.
*   **Moral Hazard:** Can obscure the true debtor (**_Qintex_**) or disadvantage involuntary creditors like tort victims (**_Briggs_**).
*   **Group Liability:**
    *   Parents cannot reorganize to avoid existing obligations.
    *   Parents may owe a duty of care to people harmed by the child company (**_CSR Ltd v Wren_**; **_Okpabi_**).
*   **Statutory Concepts:**
    *   **Subsidiary (s 46 CA):** Parent controls board composition, OR >50% votes, OR holds >50% issued share capital, OR subsidiary of another subsidiary.
    *   **Related Companies (s 50 CA):** Holding, subsidiary, or subsidiary of same holding company. Important for insolvent trading (**s 588V**), directors' duties (**s 187**), pooling assets (**s 571**).
    *   **Controlled Entities (s 50AA CA):** Capacity to determine outcomes of decisions regarding financial/operating policies.
