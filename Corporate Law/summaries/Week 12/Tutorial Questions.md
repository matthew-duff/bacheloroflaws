## Question 2

Palm Tree Coal Mining Ltd (PTCM) is a small, capitalised, professional services company providing staff and consulting services to the booming Queensland mining industry. The directors of PTCM are Max, Alexandra, and Henry, each of whom owns 20 per cent of the shares in PTCM, with the remainder traded over the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX). 

The directors of PTCM are concerned that their company’s shares seem to be trading at a discount compared to other companies in the mining services industry. They undertake a strategic review of the company’s capital structure called ‘Project Black’, the goal being to increase the share price of PTCM. Project Black involves several key transactions: 

a) The company will restructure its share capital by splitting its current shares into two classes: Class A voting shares and Class B non-voting convertible preference shares. 
b) Class A shares will have no dividend rights, with only Class B shares carrying a dividend entitlement (but no voting rights). 
c) The company will engage in a selective share buy-back to acquire 30 per cent of the company’s listed share capital. This selective buy-back will offer $2 per share for all shares purchased from the company between 2000 and 2005. The only shareholders with shares that qualify for this buy-back are Max, Alexandra and Henry, and a few minor shareholders who hold no more than 10 per cent collectively. This buy-back effectively results in Max, Alexandra and Henry (and the minority shareholders) selling back one-third of their shares to the company for $2 per share. PTCM shares have never traded on the ASX above $1.80 (their current price). 
d) Max, Alexandra, and Henry plan to use their buy-back payments to acquire Class B shares. 

The proposed buy-back has been severely criticised by the company’s institutional shareholders and by the media, as unfairly favouring Max, Alexandra, and Henry. However, as they currently hold 60 per cent of the company’s voting shares they have said that they have the power to make this change regardless of what the other shareholders think. 

You are the company secretary for PTCM and have been asked to prepare a report for the board of directors on the legal issues involved in the implementation of Project Black. Your advice should note relevant provisions of the Corporations Act and what the potential consequences may be for the company and its directors if these provisions are breached.
