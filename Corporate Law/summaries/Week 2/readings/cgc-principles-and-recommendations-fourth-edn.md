# Summary: ASX Corporate Governance Council Corporate Governance Principles and Recommendations (4th Edition, 2019)

**Issuer:** ASX Corporate Governance Council

## Core Subject
This document outlines the recommended corporate governance practices for entities listed on the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX). It is designed to achieve good governance outcomes and meet the reasonable expectations of investors, functioning on an "if not, why not" reporting basis.

## The 8 Central Principles
The guidelines are structured around eight foundational principles:

1. **Lay solid foundations for management and oversight:** Clearly delineate the respective roles and responsibilities of the board and management, and regularly review their performance.
2. **Structure the board to be effective and add value:** The board should be of an appropriate size and collectively possess the skills, commitment, and knowledge of the entity and its industry to discharge its duties effectively.
3. **Instil a culture of acting lawfully, ethically and responsibly:** The entity should instil and continually reinforce a culture of lawful, ethical, and responsible conduct across the organization.
4. **Safeguard the integrity of corporate reports:** Implement appropriate processes to verify the integrity of corporate reports, typically through an independent audit committee.
5. **Make timely and balanced disclosure:** Disclose all matters that a reasonable person would expect to have a material effect on the price or value of securities in a timely and balanced manner.
6. **Respect the rights of security holders:** Provide security holders with appropriate information and facilities to exercise their rights effectively, promoting two-way communication.
7. **Recognise and manage risk:** Establish a sound risk management framework (addressing financial and non-financial risks) and periodically review its effectiveness within the board's set risk appetite.
8. **Remunerate fairly and responsibly:** Pay director and executive remuneration sufficient to attract and retain high quality personnel, while aligning their interests with the creation of value for security holders and the entity’s values.

## Key Mechanisms
- **"If not, why not" approach:** The recommendations are not mandatory. However, under ASX Listing Rule 4.10.3, if a listed entity chooses not to adopt a recommendation, it must explain why it has not done so and what alternative governance practices it has adopted in lieu.
- **Corporate Governance Statement:** Listed entities must include a corporate governance statement in their annual report (or on their website) detailing the extent to which they have followed the recommendations during the reporting period.
