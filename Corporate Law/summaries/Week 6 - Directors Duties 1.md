# Week 6 - Directors' Duties 1: Duty of Care

## Key Statutes
* **s 9 CA**: Definitions of "director" and "officer".
* **s 180(1) CA**: Care and diligence (civil obligation only).
* **s 180(2) CA**: Business Judgment Rule defense.
* **s 189 CA**: Reliance on information or advice.
* **s 190 CA**: Responsibility for actions of delegate.
* **s 198C / 198D CA**: Delegation of powers (MD, committees, employees).
* **s 206B-F CA**: Disqualification from managing corporations.

## 1. Directors and Officers
*   **Who is a Director? (s 9)**
    1.  *De jure Director*: Validly appointed.
    2.  *De facto Director*: Not validly appointed, but acts in the position of a director (**_Grimaldi_**).
    3.  *Shadow Director*: Formal directors are accustomed to acting in accordance with their wishes (**_Buzzle Computers_**).
*   **Officers (s 9)**: People who participate in making decisions that affect a substantial part of the business, or have the capacity to significantly affect the corporation's financial standing (include Co Secs, senior execs, liquidators).
*   **Who owes duties?** Both directors and officers owe most statutory duties. Owed *to the company* as a whole (**_Percival v Wright_**), though exceptionally to individual shareholders (**_Brunninghausen v Glavanics_**).
*   **Disqualification:** Automatic (bankruptcy/convictions), Court-ordered (civil penalty breaches), or ASIC-ordered (multiple failures). Protects the public (**_ASIC v GetSwift_**).

## 2. The Duty of Care, Skill, and Diligence
*   **s 180(1) CA (and General Law):** A director/officer must exercise their powers with the degree of care and diligence that a *reasonable person* would if they were in that corporation's circumstances and occupied that office with those responsibilities.
*   **Standard is Objective:** The modern approach (**_Daniels v Anderson_**) demands all directors (including Non-Executive Directors) be in a position to guide and monitor the company.
    *   *Minimum Standards:* Obtain basic understanding of business, keep informed of activities, regularly attend board meetings, monitor financial position (**_AWA Ltd v Daniels_**).
*   **Relevant Circumstances:** **_ASIC v Rich_** dictates looking at company type/size, board composition, and the particular director's experience and specific delegated tasks.
*   **Skill:** If a director professes special skills (e.g., CFO, General Counsel), they are held to the standard of a person with those skills (**_Gold Ribbon v Sheers_**; **_Shafron v ASIC_**).
*   **Diligence:** The "silent partner" cannot escape liability by doing nothing (**_Sheahan v Verco_**).

## 3. Delegation and Reliance (ss 189, 190, 198D)
*   **s 198D:** Boards may delegate functions to committees, managing directors, employees.
*   **s 190:** Directors remain responsible for the delegate's actions unless they believed on reasonable grounds (in good faith, after proper inquiry) that the delegate was reliable/competent.
*   **s 189:** If a director relies on advice/info given by an employee, professional (lawyer/accountant), or committee, the reliance is deemed reasonable if done in good faith and after making an independent assessment.

## 4. The Business Judgment Rule (s 180(2))
*   A defense to an action for breach of the duty of care (s 180(1) and under general law). **Note:** Only applies to the Duty of Care, not other duties.
*   **Elements (Director has onus to prove):**
    1.  Made the judgment in **good faith for a proper purpose**.
    2.  Do not have a **material personal interest** in the decision.
    3.  Informed themselves about the matter to the extent they **reasonably believed to be appropriate**.
    4.  **Rationally believed** that the judgment was in the best interests of the company.

## 5. "Stepping Stones" Liability
*   A relatively new form of liability where a company's breach of law is used as a "stepping stone" to holding directors personally liable for breaching their duty of care (s 180(1)) by exposing the company to the risk of that breach (**_ASIC v Fortescue Metals_**; **_ASIC v Cassimatis (No 8)_**; **_ASIC v Mariner Corp_**).
