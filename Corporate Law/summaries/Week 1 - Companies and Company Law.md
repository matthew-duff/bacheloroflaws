# Week 1: Companies and Company Law

## 📝 Overview & Core Concepts
A company is an artificial legal person created by law to hold assets and carry on a business or other activity separate from its participants (investors, managers). In legal terms, it is a type of body corporate.

- **Registration:** A company is an association of people who form a separate legal entity by complying with registration requirements under the **Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)**.
- **Key Provision:** **s 119** (Company comes into existence on registration).

## 🏢 Business Structures Comparison
Different structures offer varied benefits based on liability, continuity, and compliance.

### The Company Advantage
- **Limited Liability:** Risk transfer and management.
- **Perpetual Succession:** Business change and continuity independent of individuals.
- **Transferability of Ownership:** Business growth and change (shares can be sold).
- **Unlimited Investors:** Access to capital (especially for public companies).
- **Contractual Capacity:** Can act like an individual but broader.
- **Internal Rules:** Familiar, predetermined rules.

### Alternative Structures
Why choose another structure?
- Lower **compliance costs** (establishing and operating).
- Less **disclosure**, providing more privacy for owners/managers.
- Avoid strict enforcement of the *Corporations Act* requirements.

Other common structures include:
- **Sole trader:** One-person, unincorporated.
- **(Un)incorporated association:** Non-profit goals.
- **Partnerships:** Relation between 2+ persons carrying on a business with a view to profit (**s 5 Partnership Act 1891 (Qld)**; max 20 partners under **s 115 CA 2001**).
- **Trust:** Trustee holds legal title for a beneficiary.
- **Joint Venture:** Contractual agreement to cooperate on a venture.

## 🗂 Classifying Companies
Companies are primarily classified by **members' liability** and **size/public status**.

### 1. By Members' Liability (s 9)
- **Limited by Shares:** Members' liability limited to unpaid amount on shares.
- **Limited by Guarantee:** Members agree to contribute a set amount if the company winds up (often used for non-profits).
- **Unlimited Companies:** Members have unlimited liability.
- **No Liability Companies:** Mining companies where members have no statutory liability to pay unpaid calls on shares.

### 2. Proprietary vs. Public Companies
| Feature | Proprietary (Pty Ltd) (s 112/113/148/45A) | Public (Ltd) (s 112) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shareholders** | Min 1, Max 50 | Min 1, No maximum |
| **Directors/Officers** | Min 1 director (must reside in AU) | Min 3 directors (2 in AU); Min 1 secretary |
| **Fundraising** | Cannot raise funds from the general public (Ch 6D restrictions) | Can raise funds from public (with disclosure document) |
| **Listing** | Cannot be listed on ASX | Can be listed or unlisted |

### 3. Small vs. Large Proprietary Companies (s 45A)
A proprietary company is "small" if it satisfies at least 2 of the following 3 tests:
1. Revenue < $50 million
2. Assets < $25 million
3. Employees < 100
*Note: Large proprietary companies have stricter financial reporting and disclosure obligations (e.g., s 292).*

## ⚖️ Pre-Registration & Promoters
- **Promoters:** Hold a position of power and influence, thus owing **fiduciary duties** to the company being formed.
- **Pre-Registration Contracts (s 131):** Since a company lacks capacity before it exists, **s 131** governs conditions and effects of contracts made on behalf of an unformed company, including liability for breach.

## 🏛 Legal Framework & Context (Company Law)
Company law governs corporate formation, attributes, duties, and financing.

### Sources of Law
- **Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)** & Regulations
- Case Law (Precedents)
- ASIC Act
- Accounting Standards & ASX Listing Rules

### Key Historical & Constitutional Cases
- *Salomon v A Salomon & Co Ltd [1897]*: Foundational case establishing the **Separate Legal Entity** principle.
- *Lee v Lee’s Air Farming Ltd [1961]*: Reinforces the corporate veil concept.
- Constitutional basis heavily relies on cases like *NSW v Commonwealth (1990)* and the referral powers leading to the unified *Corporations Act 2001*.

## 📖 Theoretical Perspectives (The "Why")
Company law isn't just rules; it's a product of social and economic choices.
- **Mainstream View:** Focuses on investor protection, commercial stability, and legal certainty.
- **Microeconomic View:** Law is shaped by individual decisions to maximize efficiency/utility.
- **Socio-Legal & Critical Views:** Law is a social process shaped by power relations, challenging traditional winners/losers and alternative measures of success (e.g., corporate social responsibility).
