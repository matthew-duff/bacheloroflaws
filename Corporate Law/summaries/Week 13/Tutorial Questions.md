## Question 2

Great Escapes Pty Ltd (GEPL) is in the adventure tourism business. Its principal customers are people in their early 20s who want to explore beautiful and rugged locations in South-East Asia. Lola is its Managing Director. Gary is its Chief Financial Officer (CFO). 

GEPL managed to survive the pandemic through a restructure after a period of voluntary administration. However, since it returned to ordinary business, it has struggled to adjust to the post-pandemic business environment, in which both customers and airlines cancel trips much more readily. GEPL has also struggled to pay the interests on its loans, since they became unfixed late last year. Since February, it has been arrears on the rent of two out of ten of its shop; the landlords are commencing action to recover the premise. In March, GEPL fell behind in its repayments to Big Bank, which is a major secured creditor. 

Seeing the problems in the company, Gary decided that he should move quickly to make sure that GEPL’s ‘house is in order’ if a liquidator is appointed. In April, he arranges for contracts with the tour business of his best friend, Kel, to be paid in full, and a ‘Going Out in Style’ retirement party for Nancy, the company’s long-serving and much-loved accountant. GEPL pays $100,000 to an event planning company run by Gary’s daughter to organise the event, which includes a waterslide and a bar tab for 300 hundred people all night. 

Presume GEPL was wound up in insolvency at the start of May. Advise the liquidator.
