# Summary: Wilson, ‘Salomon’s Case deters Attempts to lift the Veil’ (1961)

## Overview
This article discusses the Privy Council decision in **Lee v Lee’s Air Farming Ltd [1961] AC 12**, framing it as a strong reaffirmation of the "separate legal entity" principle established in **Salomon v Salomon & Co Ltd [1897] AC 22**.

## Key Case: Lee v Lee’s Air Farming Ltd [1961] AC 12

### Facts
- **Geoffrey Lee** formed a company, **Lee’s Air Farming Ltd**, for aerial top-dressing.
- He held **2,999 of the 3,000 shares** (the remaining share held by a solicitor).
- He was the **sole governing director** with full control.
- He was also appointed by the articles as the **chief pilot** (a "worker" under a contract of service).
- Lee was killed while piloting for the company. His widow claimed workers' compensation.

### Legal Issue
Can a person who is the sole governing director and majority shareholder of a company also be a "worker" (servant) of that same company under a contract of service?

### Ratio Decidendi (The Principles)
The Privy Council (per Lord Morris) held that Lee was a worker, establishing three key propositions:

1.  **Separate Legal Entity:** A company is a distinct legal persona, not a "sham" or "simulacrum," even if controlled by one person.
2.  **Contractual Capacity:** The company’s separate existence allows it to enter into valid contracts with its members or directors.
3.  **Dual Capacity:** A person can act in two distinct capacities:
    - As **agent** of the company (e.g., as director, entering into a contract on the company's behalf).
    - As **servant/worker** of the company (e.g., as chief pilot, performing the work).
    - *Crucially:* The fact that Lee gave himself orders (as director to pilot) does not negate the master-servant relationship between the company and himself.

## Key Arguments & Analysis
- **Rigid Adherence to Salomon:** The court rejected the "logical" argument that one cannot be both master and servant. It emphasized that once the company is legally incorporated, its transactions must be treated as valid.
- **Lifting the Veil Refused:** The court refused to "lift the veil" to look at the reality of Lee's total control. Instead, it strictly applied the corporate entity principle to ensure the widow received statutory compensation.
- **Policy Implication:** The decision shows that the separate legal entity doctrine is applied even when it leads to "novel" results (like a man being his own employer), provided the company is not a sham.

## Exam Revision Summary
- **Primary Authority:** *Lee v Lee’s Air Farming Ltd* (reaffirming *Salomon*).
- **Core Principle:** One-man companies are valid legal entities capable of contracting with their sole controller.
- **Key Concept:** **Dual Capacity** — A director can be an employee of their own company.
- **Quote to Remember:** "It is impossible to deny the validity of the transactions into which [the company] has entered." (Lord Halsbury, cited by the Privy Council).
