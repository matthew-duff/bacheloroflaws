# Case Summary: Lee v Lee’s Air Farming Ltd [1961] AC 12

## **Overview**
*   **Court:** Privy Council (on appeal from New Zealand)
*   **Key Parties:** Catherine Lee (Appellant/Widow) v Lee’s Air Farming Ltd (Respondent)
*   **Core Legal Issue:** Can a person be both the controlling shareholder/governing director of a company and its "worker" (servant) under a contract of service?

---

## **Facts**
1.  **Incorporation:** Geoffrey Lee formed "Lee’s Air Farming Ltd" to conduct an aerial top-dressing business.
2.  **Control:** Lee held 2,999 of the 3,000 shares and was appointed **Governing Director for life** with full "government and control" of the company.
3.  **Employment:** The Articles of Association (Art 33) stipulated the company "shall employ" Lee as **chief pilot**.
4.  **The Accident:** Lee died in an aircraft crash while performing top-dressing operations for the company.
5.  **Claim:** His widow claimed workers' compensation. The company (insurer) argued Lee was not a "worker" because he was the one giving himself orders (i.e., you cannot be both master and servant).

---

## **Ratio Decidendi**
The Privy Council held that **a company is a separate legal entity distinct from its controllers**, and therefore:
1.  **Dual Capacity:** One person can function in dual capacities (e.g., as a director/agent of the company and as an employee/servant of the company).
2.  **Contractual Validity:** A valid contract of service can exist between a company and its sole governing director. The company (as a separate person) is the "master" and the individual is the "servant."
3.  **Right of Control:** The "right of control" resides in the company as a legal entity. Even if the individual exercises that control *on behalf of* the company, the legal relationship of employer and employee remains intact.

---

## **Key Principles & Legal Significance**
*   **Extension of *Salomon v Salomon*:** This case is the primary authority for applying the *Salomon* principle (separate legal personality) to the context of employment and "one-man companies."
*   **Rejection of "Sham" Argument:** Unless the company is a mere "simulacrum" or "sham" (which it wasn't here), its capacity to contract with its members/directors cannot be impugned.
*   **Corporate Agency:** When Lee "appointed himself" as pilot, he was acting as the **agent** of the company. The contract was between the Company (Principal) and Lee (Employee).
*   **Exam Tip:** Use this case to counter arguments that a "one-man company" is just an alias for the individual. It proves that the corporate veil creates a functional legal barrier allowing for internal contracts.

---

## **Quick Revision Summary**
*   **The Rule:** A controlling director can be an employee of their own company.
*   **The Logic:** Company = Person A; Director = Person B. Person A can hire Person B.
*   **The Result:** Mrs. Lee was entitled to compensation because Mr. Lee was a "worker" at the time of death.
