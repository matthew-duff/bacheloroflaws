# Tutorial 5: Building on Content from Lecture 6 (Directors’ Duties 1, The Duty of Care and Diligence)

Swipe Ltd (Swipe) is a listed public company in the technology business. It has had great success by developing and marketing a technology for securely holding sensitive personal data (e.g., customer details, tax file numbers, credit card information etc). Swipe’s customers include major health insurance companies and banks. 

Swift has four directors: David, Sharon, Todd, Fred and Melissa. David and Sharon are both also employed in the business, David as Chief Technology Officer and Managing Director, and Sharon as Chief Financial Officer and Chair of the Board. Todd, Fred and Melissa are independent non-executive directors with backgrounds in the technology and data management business. 

Roger was a director and CEO of Swipe but resigned early last year to pursue other business opportunities. However, Roger remains the single largest investor in Swift and has significant influence over Swift’s business. Among other things, David and Sharon still report to Roger on Swift’s activities every week over informal ‘coffee catch-ups’. Roger is a notoriously commanding personality and both David and Sharon find it quite difficult to challenge his opinions on how they should run the company on a day-today basis. They do not decide any issues of importance without first consulting Roger and asking for his approval. 

In January this year, at Todd, Fred and Melissa’s urging, David commissioned an internal investigation into the security of Swipe’s technology products. The report was authored by Swipe’s top engineers and found that Swipe’s major product had a significant vulnerability to hacking. The vulnerability meant the personal data held by Swipe’s customers could be accessed and published by hackers. The report said that such attack would be ‘catastrophic’ for Swipe’s business. 

When David receives the report in March, he immediately forwards it to Roger and who tells him to ‘bury it’. David therefore pays the engineers who conducted the investigations to leave the company and tells the board, at their next meeting, that the engineers were dismissed for misconduct before they could hand in their findings. Todd, Fred and Melissa question David closely about this claim but choose to accept David’s version of events when Sharon tries to close down the discussion of that agenda item. (Sharon is not interested in the technical part of the business and so is keen for the meeting to move on to other matters). 

Swipe’s major product is hacked and sensitive data stolen from one of its banking customers. Swipe’s share price crashes in light of the bad publicity and the civil lawsuits and regulatory actions it now faces. 

Advise Swipe on issues relating to breach of the duty of care.
